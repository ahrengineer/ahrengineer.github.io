My Expertise: Swept Path Analysis

Overview of Swept Path Analysis Proficiency

Swept path analysis is a critical skill in transportation engineering, ensuring that proposed designs can safely and efficiently accommodate the turning movements of various vehicle types. My expertise in this area allows me to evaluate and validate geometric layouts for roads, intersections, parking lots, and loading areas.

Key Applications & Capabilities

My proficiency in swept path analysis is applied to:

  • Intersection Design Review: Assessing the adequacy of turning radii and intersection geometry for standard and oversized vehicles.
  • Parking Lot Design: Ensuring proper circulation and maneuverability within parking facilities for cars, trucks, and buses.
  • Loading Dock & Service Area Design: Verifying that service vehicles (e.g., delivery trucks, waste collection vehicles) can access and maneuver within designated areas without conflict.
  • Bus Terminal & Depot Design: Evaluating bus movements, including entry, exit, and turning within terminals and depots.
  • Emergency Vehicle Access: Confirming that fire trucks, ambulances, and other emergency vehicles can navigate the proposed infrastructure.
  • Roundabout Analysis: Checking the swept paths of design vehicles through roundabout entries, circulatory roadways, and exits.
  • Construction Site Access: Planning and verifying access routes for large construction vehicles and equipment.

Methodology & Tools

My approach to swept path analysis involves:

  • Identifying the appropriate design vehicles based on project requirements and local regulations (e.g., passenger cars, single-unit trucks, articulated vehicles, buses).
  • Utilizing industry-standard software to simulate the turning movements of these vehicles.
  • Overlaying the swept path envelopes onto proposed geometric designs in AutoCAD to identify potential conflicts with curbs, signs, buildings, or other infrastructure elements.
  • Proposing necessary geometric adjustments (e.g., widening lanes, increasing turning radii, modifying island shapes) to resolve identified conflicts.
  • Documenting the analysis and recommendations with clear diagrams and reports.

My extensive experience with AutoCAD allows me to effectively utilize and interpret swept path analysis outputs, ensuring practical and compliant design solutions for a wide range of transportation projects.
